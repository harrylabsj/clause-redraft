# Clause Redraft

## Use Scenarios

### Scenario 1: One-Sided Indemnification Clause
A startup founder receives a service agreement from a large enterprise client containing a clause that requires the startup to indemnify the client for "any and all claims arising from or related to this agreement, regardless of fault." **What the skill does:** Identifies the unlimited and fault-agnostic nature of the clause, generates 2-3 alternative versions (balanced mutual indemnification limited to negligence or willful misconduct, protective version with capped liability and fault-based trigger, plain-language version that clarifies scope), and explains what each version changes and why. **Expected outcome:** The founder can negotiate from an informed position with concrete alternative language that limits exposure to reasonable commercial risk.

### Scenario 2: Ambiguous Payment Milestone
A freelance developer's contract states payment is "due upon satisfactory completion of development work," but "satisfactory" is undefined, leading to repeated payment delays from the client. **What the skill does:** Analyzes the ambiguity risk (subjective standard, no objective trigger), generates alternatives including payment tied to defined deliverable acceptance criteria, milestone-based payments with specific dates, and a combination with objective acceptance/rejection procedures and timelines. **Expected outcome:** A clear, objectively verifiable payment clause that eliminates subjective interpretation and ensures timely compensation upon measurable completion.

### Scenario 3: Overbroad Non-Compete Clause
An employee receives an employment contract with a non-compete clause that restricts working for "any competitor in any capacity anywhere in the world for 5 years" after leaving. **What the skill does:** Flags the likely enforceability issues (excessive duration, geographic scope, and breadth), redrafts to reasonable scope consistent with legal standards (12-24 months, specific geographic market, compensated during restriction period), and provides a simple summary version for negotiation discussion. **Expected outcome:** A narrowed, more enforceable non-compete clause that protects legitimate business interests without unreasonably restricting the employee's future career.

## Overview

This skill helps users rewrite unclear, ambiguous, or potentially risky contract clauses into clearer, more balanced language. It provides alternative wording while preserving the intended meaning.

**⚠️ Important Disclaimer**: This tool provides drafting assistance only. It does not constitute legal advice, nor does it replace professional legal counsel. Always consult a qualified attorney before finalizing contract language.

## When to Use This Skill

- A clause seems unclear or ambiguous
- Language feels one-sided or unbalanced
- You want more professional contract wording
- Translating informal agreements into formal contract language

## Redraft Approaches

| Approach | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Balanced | Fair to both parties, clear obligations, reasonable protections |
| Protective | Stronger protections for your interests, still enforceable |
| Simple | Plain language, removes unnecessary legalese |

## Common Redraft Scenarios

| Area | Example Issue |
|------|--------------|
| Payment Terms | Vague due dates, unclear triggers |
| Termination | Unilateral termination without notice or cause |
| Confidentiality | Perpetual obligations, undefined scope |
| Liability | Unlimited liability, one-sided disclaimers |
| IP Ownership | Ambiguous assignment, unclear license scope |

## Usage

### Basic Redraft
- "帮我改写这个条款" / "这句话怎么写更清楚" / "优化一下这个条款"

### With Context
- "我是乙方，帮我改一下这个条款" / "这个条款太偏向甲方了，改平衡一点" / "用简单的话重说一遍"

## Output Format

For each request: original clause, issues identified, 2-3 alternative versions (balanced, protective, simple), with explanations of changes and a recommendation.

## Limitations

- Provides suggested alternatives only, not definitive language
- Cannot assess enforceability under specific jurisdictions
- Not a substitute for professional legal drafting
